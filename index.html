Interesting facts about camel

  1. Camel's ears are furry
  2. Camels can move easily across sand because of its specially designed feet
  3. When they find water they will drink as much as possible

Interesting facts about chameleon

  1. Chameleons are reptiles that are part of iguana suborder
  2. Changing skin is an important part of of communication among chameleon
  3. Most chameleons have prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Interesting facts about cicada

  1. . Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs. They are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
  2. They’re true bugs (from the order Hemiptera). So you can call them a bug and be scientifically accurate.
  3. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes. Keep on the lookout.

Interesting facts about pigeon

  1. Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals. They are one of only a small number of species to pass the ‘mirror test’ – a test of self recognition
  2. Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  3. Pigeons mate for life, and tend to raise two chicks at the same time.

Interesting facts about bear

  1. Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio; and use tools in various contexts from play to hunting.
  2. Some species of Asiatic bear build nests in the trees. They can use these for hiding, eating and even sleeping.
  3. Bears care deeply about family members. They will risk their lives and even fight to the death in order to save a cub or sibling from danger.
